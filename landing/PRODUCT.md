# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Hungry people who want real, homemade food from cooks in their own neighborhood — and are tired of chain restaurants and ghost-kitchen delivery apps. Preppa hasn't launched in most places yet, so the visitor usually can't order today; their job on this page is to join the waitlist and remember Preppa when it goes live near them. A secondary visitor is a prospective cook (a "Prepper") sizing up whether to sell their food here.

## Product Purpose

Preppa is a marketplace for homemade food from verified local cooks — weekly meal prep, meal plans, catering, and food experiences. This surface is the pre-launch marketing landing page; success is a waitlist signup (and, secondarily, cook interest).

## Positioning

Real food from real local cooks — the anti-chain, anti-ghost-kitchen. Homemade, human, and neighborhood-scale, not a delivery app reselling restaurants.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: Get early access — the waitlist email capture (with optional ZIP).
- Secondary CTA: for prospective cooks — learn about cooking on Preppa (links to help.preppa.live, not the app).
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: real homemade food from cooks on your block, coming soon.
- Belief ladder: (1) this is genuinely homemade food, not restaurants or a ghost kitchen; (2) it comes from actual verified people near me; (3) it's worth getting in early for.
- Proof on hand: 8 real verified kitchens across 6 cuisines (West African, Soul food, Oaxacan, Halal & Desi, Italian comfort, Healthy & seafood). Pre-launch — proof is thin on purpose. Never fabricate stats, counts, testimonials, or ratings.

## Brand Personality

Warm, bold, appetite-first, and a little irreverent — the voice of a confident neighborhood cook, not a startup. Three words: appetizing, human, bold. It should make you hungry and make you smile, and feel like a real person made it. Modern food-brand energy (Graza, Omsom, Oatly) delivered with editorial craft: typography and food do the talking.

## Anti-references

Not cold, corporate, soulless, or safe — the single biggest thing to avoid. Not a generic tech/SaaS startup page (gradients, glassmorphism, chip rows, phone mockups, tiny uppercase eyebrows — the AI defaults this page previously leaned on). Not a food-delivery app (DoorDash/Uber Eats busyness, coupons, badges). Not the editorial-magazine serif cliché (Playfair/Fraunces italic + hairline rules) — editorial as *feeling*, not that saturated aesthetic.

## Design Principles

Appetite first — real food, shown big and mouth-watering, does most of the persuading. Human, not corporate — a real neighbor's voice and hand beats startup polish. Type with attitude — the headline is a lead actor, characterful and confident, never a neutral label. Commit to color — own a warm, saturated palette; no beige hedging. Stay honest — real cooks and real cuisines only; a pre-launch page earns trust by not faking scale.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA contrast for text over color and imagery, honor prefers-reduced-motion (static poster instead of the food video, no decorative loops), full keyboard access with visible focus, and 44px+ touch targets.
